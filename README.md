# OriginTag

**The craft communicates its own worth — so the artisan never has to.**

OriginTag is a design prototype built during a 9-day Design Sprint at IIM Udaipur (March 2026), facilitated by TinkerLabs. It addresses a real problem in the Udaipur craft economy: skilled artisans who price their work honestly and create extraordinary things — but whose customers walk away without understanding what they're holding.

---

## The Problem

Udaipur's miniature painters are 3rd and 4th generation artists. They price by the day (₹850/day for a master). They do not negotiate — a lowball offer is an insult to the hours, not a price discussion. But the tourist standing in front of a ₹17,850 painting has no context. They see an object. They don't see 21 days.

The artisan's identity as an artist makes pitching feel like a betrayal of their craft. So they stay silent. The tourist walks away. The work sits.

**The irrationality:** *"I shouldn't, but I'd rather let my work sit unsold than justify its price to someone who doesn't already understand it."*

**The diagnosis:** Messenger Effect — their artist identity makes them incapable of being the one to assert value.

**The need:** Respect — they are acutely conscious of how they are seen, not by the tourist, but by their craft and their lineage.

---

## The Solution

A QR code placed beside each artwork. The tourist scans it. A page opens that takes them through the painting — what it means, what's inside it, how it was made, who made it, and where similar work has gone. By the time they look up from their phone, they already know what they're holding.

**The artisan never has to say a word. The work speaks for itself.**

---

## The Five Features

1. **The Hours** — days invested, daily rate, shown so the price makes honest sense
2. **The Making** — the painting's journey from blank paper to finished work, in stages
3. **The Maker** — the artist's lineage, how long they've practiced, the thought behind this piece
4. **The Journey** — provenance record, where similar work has travelled
5. **One of Few** — how many significant works this artist makes per year

---

## This Prototype

This repository contains a single-file prototype (`index.html`) built for **Pacific Art, Hathi Pol Bazaar, Udaipur** — a studio of 26 artists across 3 generations, Est. 1952.

The prototype is built for the painting **"Udaipur ki Shaam"** — a Rajasthani miniature depicting the Maharana at rest in his lakeside pavilion, watching the sun set behind the Lake Palace. 21 days. Natural pigments and 24-karat gold leaf. One of 14 works made in 2024.

All images were generated using Google Gemini Image Generation based on detailed prompts derived from field research conducted in Udaipur on March 22, 2026.

---

## Design Decisions

- **No price in the hero** — the tourist earns the price by going through the journey
- **No QR on the page** — if you're already here, you've already scanned
- **Gallery name used sparingly** — once at the start, once at the close
- **Effort shown through stages, not stated upfront** — the tourist watches 21 days unfold, they don't need to be told
- **Price at the very end, small and quiet** — the way a gallery would present it

---

## How to Use

1. Host this file on GitHub Pages
2. Generate a unique QR code pointing to the live URL
3. Print the QR on a small card (A6, cream card stock)
4. Place the card beside the artwork in the gallery
5. Tourist scans → page opens → value is felt before price is seen

For each new artwork, duplicate the file, update the content, generate a new QR. One page, one painting, one QR.
